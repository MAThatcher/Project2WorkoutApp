Informational Page

This page should help aid you in your search for the right workout routine. There are also external resources provided for your convenience.

Training Type #1
Continuous training develops cardiovascular fitness

Swimming, running, cycling, walking, or a combination of these disciplines.

Learn more
Training Type #2
Speed Play Training

Changes of speed, incline, and terrain are used to provide changes in exercise intensity. Aerobic and anaerobic work can be done in quantities that suit the performer.

Learn more
Training Type #3
Interval training develops strength, speed and muscular endurance

Periods of intense work interspersed with timed rest. Structured in reps and sets.

Learn more
Training Type #4
Weight training develops strength

Interval form of training. Time is structured in reps and sets with specific timings for recovery between sets.

Learn more
Training Type #5
Plyometric training develops power

High intensity exercise invovling explosive movements. Muscle is lengthened and the rapidly shortened to develop the explosive capability of the muscle.

Learn more
Training Type #6
Flexibility training develops flexibility

Essential for all athletes in all sports and activities. Time is measured by the length of hold and recovery period between holds. Intensity is measured as a percentage of range of motion.

Learn more
Training Type #7
Circuit training

Stations are set out that train one or more components of fitness. The performer moves from one station to the next with exercise periods and rest periods.

Learn more
Training Type #8
Yoga, pilates, body pump, and spinning

Very popular exercise formats. Group formats help to motivate participants to work harder. Outstanding for developing core strenght and whole body conditioning.
